70.1 More about Tables of Marks

Let G 𝐺 G be a finite group with n 𝑛 n conjugacy classes of subgroups C 1 , C 2 , … , C n subscript 𝐶 1 subscript 𝐶 2 … subscript 𝐶 𝑛 C_{1},C_{2},\ldots,C_{n} and representatives H i ∈ C i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 subscript 𝐶 𝑖 H_{i}\in C_{i} , 1 ≤ i ≤ n 1 𝑖 𝑛 1\leq i\leq n . The table of marks of G 𝐺 G is defined to be the n × n 𝑛 𝑛 n\times n matrix M = ( m i ⁢ j ) 𝑀 subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑗 M=(m_{ij}) where the mark m i ⁢ j subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑗 m_{ij} is the number of fixed points of the subgroup H j subscript 𝐻 𝑗 H_{j} in the action of G 𝐺 G on the right cosets of H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} in G 𝐺 G .

Since H j subscript 𝐻 𝑗 H_{j} can only have fixed points if it is contained in a point stabilizer the matrix M 𝑀 M is lower triangular if the classes C i subscript 𝐶 𝑖 C_{i} are sorted according to the condition that if H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} is contained in a conjugate of H j subscript 𝐻 𝑗 H_{j} then i ≤ j 𝑖 𝑗 i\leq j .

Moreover, the diagonal entries m i ⁢ i subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑖 m_{ii} are nonzero since m i ⁢ i subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑖 m_{ii} equals the index of H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} in its normalizer in G 𝐺 G . Hence M 𝑀 M is invertible. Since any transitive action of G 𝐺 G is equivalent to an action on the cosets of a subgroup of G 𝐺 G , one sees that the table of marks completely characterizes the set of all permutation representations of G 𝐺 G .

The marks m i ⁢ j subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑗 m_{ij} have further meanings. If H 1 subscript 𝐻 1 H_{1} is the trivial subgroup of G 𝐺 G then each mark m i ⁢ 1 subscript 𝑚 𝑖 1 m_{i1} in the first column of M 𝑀 M is equal to the index of H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} in G 𝐺 G since the trivial subgroup fixes all cosets of H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} . If H n = G subscript 𝐻 𝑛 𝐺 H_{n}=G then each m n ⁢ j subscript 𝑚 𝑛 𝑗 m_{nj} in the last row of M 𝑀 M is equal to 1 1 1 since there is only one coset of G 𝐺 G in G 𝐺 G . In general, m i ⁢ j subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑗 m_{ij} equals the number of conjugates of H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} containing H j subscript 𝐻 𝑗 H_{j} , multiplied by the index of H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} in its normalizer in G 𝐺 G . Moreover, the number c i ⁢ j subscript 𝑐 𝑖 𝑗 c_{ij} of conjugates of H j subscript 𝐻 𝑗 H_{j} which are contained in H i subscript 𝐻 𝑖 H_{i} can be derived from the marks m i ⁢ j subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑗 m_{ij} via the formula

\[ c_{ij} = ( m_{ij} m_{j1} ) / ( m_{i1} m_{jj} ) \]

.

Both the marks m i ⁢ j subscript 𝑚 𝑖 𝑗 m_{ij} and the numbers of subgroups c i ⁢ j subscript 𝑐 𝑖 𝑗 c_{ij} are needed for the functions described in this chapter.
