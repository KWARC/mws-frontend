64.2-6 RightDerivations

These functions all return the matrix Lie algebra of derivations of the algebra A 𝐴 A with basis B.

RightDerivations( B ) returns the algebra of derivations represented by their right action on the algebra A 𝐴 A . This means that with respect to the basis B 𝐵 B of A 𝐴 A , the derivation D 𝐷 D is described by the matrix [ d i , j ] delimited-[] subscript 𝑑 𝑖 𝑗 [d_{{i,j}}] which means that D 𝐷 D maps the i 𝑖 i -th basis element b i subscript 𝑏 𝑖 b_{i} to ∑ j = 1 n d i , j ⁢ b j superscript subscript 𝑗 1 𝑛 subscript 𝑑 𝑖 𝑗 subscript 𝑏 𝑗 \sum_{{j=1}}^{n}d_{{i,j}}b_{j} .

LeftDerivations( B ) returns the Lie algebra of derivations represented by their left action on the algebra A 𝐴 A . So the matrices contained in the algebra output by LeftDerivations( B ) are the transposes of the matrices contained in the output of RightDerivations( B ).

Derivations is just a synonym for RightDerivations.
