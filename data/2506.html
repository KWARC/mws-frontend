25.5-2 LLLReducedGramMat
‣ LLLReducedGramMat( G[, y] )( function )

LLLReducedGramMat provides an implementation of the LLL algorithm by Lenstra, Lenstra and Lovász (see [LLL82], [Poh87]). The implementation follows the description in [Coh93, p. 94f.].

Let G the Gram matrix of the vectors ( b 1 , b 2 , … , b n ) subscript 𝑏 1 subscript 𝑏 2 … subscript 𝑏 𝑛 (b_{1},b_{2},\ldots,b_{n}) ; this means G is either a square symmetric matrix or lower triangular matrix (only the entries in the lower triangular half are used by the program).

LLLReducedGramMat returns a record whose component remainder is the Gram matrix of the LLL reduced basis corresponding to ( b 1 , b 2 , … , b n ) subscript 𝑏 1 subscript 𝑏 2 … subscript 𝑏 𝑛 (b_{1},b_{2},\ldots,b_{n}) . If G is a lower triangular matrix then also the remainder component of the result record is a lower triangular matrix.

The result record contains also the components relations and transformation, which have the following meaning.

relations is a basis of the space of vectors ( x 1 , x 2 , … , x n ) subscript 𝑥 1 subscript 𝑥 2 … subscript 𝑥 𝑛 (x_{1},x_{2},\ldots,x_{n}) such that ∑ i = 1 n x i ⁢ b i superscript subscript 𝑖 1 𝑛 subscript 𝑥 𝑖 subscript 𝑏 𝑖 \sum_{{i=1}}^{n}x_{i}b_{i} is zero, and transformation gives the expression of the new lattice basis in terms of the old, i.e., transformation is the matrix T 𝑇 T such that T ⋅ 𝐺 ⋅ T t ⁢ r ⋅ 𝑇 G superscript 𝑇 𝑡 𝑟 T\cdot\textit{G}\cdot T^{tr} is the remainder component of the result.

The optional argument y denotes the "sensitivity" of the algorithm, it must be a rational number between 1 / 4 1 4 1/4 and 1 1 1 ; the default value is 𝑦 = 3 / 4 y 3 4 \textit{y}=3/4 .
