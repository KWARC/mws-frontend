72.9 Restricted and Induced Class Functions

For restricting a class function of a group G 𝐺 G to a subgroup H 𝐻 H and for inducing a class function of H 𝐻 H to G 𝐺 G , the class fusion from H 𝐻 H to G 𝐺 G must be known (see 73.3).

If F 𝐹 F is the factor group of G 𝐺 G by the normal subgroup N 𝑁 N then each class function of F 𝐹 F can be naturally regarded as a class function of G 𝐺 G , with N 𝑁 N in its kernel. For a class function of F 𝐹 F , the corresponding class function of G 𝐺 G is called the inflated class function. Restriction and inflation are in principle the same, namely indirection of a class function by the appropriate fusion map, and thus no extra operation is needed for this process. But note that contrary to the case of a subgroup fusion, the factor fusion can in general not be computed from the groups G 𝐺 G and F 𝐹 F ; either one needs the natural homomorphism, or the factor fusion to the character table of F 𝐹 F must be stored on the table of G 𝐺 G . This explains the different syntax for computing restricted and inflated class functions.

In the following, the meaning of the optional first argument tbl is the same as in Section 72.8.
