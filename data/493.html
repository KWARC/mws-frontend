71.14 Computing the Irreducible Characters of a Group

Several algorithms are available for computing the irreducible characters of a finite group G 𝐺 G . The default method for arbitrary finite groups is to use the Dixon-Schneider algorithm (see IrrDixonSchneider (71.14-1)). For supersolvable groups, Conlon's algorithm can be used (see IrrConlon (71.14-2)). For abelian-by-supersolvable groups, the Baum-Clausen algorithm for computing the irreducible representations (see IrreducibleRepresentations (71.14-4)) can be used to compute the irreducible characters (see IrrBaumClausen (71.14-3)).

These functions are installed in methods for Irr (71.8-2), but explicitly calling one of them will not set the Irr (71.8-2) value of G 𝐺 G .
