49.4 Wreath Products

The wreath product of a group G 𝐺 G with a permutation group P 𝑃 P acting on n 𝑛 n points is the semidirect product of the normal subgroup 𝐺 n superscript G 𝑛 \textit{G}^{n} with the group P 𝑃 P which acts on 𝐺 n superscript G 𝑛 \textit{G}^{n} by permuting the components.

Note that GAP always considers the domain of a permutation group to be the points moved by elements of the group as returned by MovedPoints (42.3-3), i.e. it is not possible to have a domain to include fixed points, I.e. P = ⟨ ( 1 , 2 , 3 ) ⟩ 𝑃 delimited-⟨⟩ 1 2 3 P=\langle(1,2,3)\rangle and P = ⟨ ( 1 , 3 , 5 ) ⟩ 𝑃 delimited-⟨⟩ 1 3 5 P=\langle(1,3,5)\rangle result in isomorphic wreath products. (If fixed points are desired the wreath product G ≀ T ≀ 𝐺 𝑇 G\wr T has to be formed with a transitive overgroup T 𝑇 T of P 𝑃 P and then the pre-image of P 𝑃 P under the projection G ≀ T → T → ≀ 𝐺 𝑇 𝑇 G\wr T\rightarrow T has to be taken.)
