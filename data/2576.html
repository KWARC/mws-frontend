15 Number Theory

GAP provides a couple of elementary number theoretic functions. Most of these deal with the group of integers coprime to m 𝑚 m , called the prime residue group. The order of this group is ϕ ⁢ ( m ) italic-ϕ 𝑚 \phi(m) (see Phi (15.2-2)), and λ ⁢ ( m ) 𝜆 𝑚 \lambda(m) (see Lambda (15.2-3)) is its exponent. This group is cyclic if and only if m 𝑚 m is 2, 4, an odd prime power p n superscript 𝑝 𝑛 p^{n} , or twice an odd prime power 2 ⁢ p n 2 superscript 𝑝 𝑛 2p^{n} . In this case the generators of the group, i.e., elements of order ϕ ⁢ ( m ) italic-ϕ 𝑚 \phi(m) , are called primitive roots (see PrimitiveRootMod (15.3-3)).

Note that neither the arguments nor the return values of the functions listed below are groups or group elements in the sense of GAP. The arguments are simply integers.
