39.3 Subgroups

For the general concept of parents and subdomains, see 31.7 and 31.8. More functions that construct certain subgroups can be found in the sections 39.11, 39.12, 39.13, and 39.14.

If a group U 𝑈 U is created as a subgroup of another group G 𝐺 G , G 𝐺 G becomes the parent of U 𝑈 U . There is no "universal" parent group, parent-child chains can be arbitrary long. GAP stores the result of some operations (such as Normalizer (39.11-1)) with the parent as an attribute.
