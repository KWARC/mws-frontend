40.6-3 InnerAutomorphism
‣ InnerAutomorphism( G, g )( function )
‣ InnerAutomorphismNC( G, g )( operation )

Let G be a group, and 𝑔 ∈ 𝐺 g G \textit{g}\in\textit{G} . InnerAutomorphism returns the automorphism of G defined by h ↦ h 𝑔 maps-to ℎ superscript ℎ g h\mapsto h^{\textit{g}} for all h ∈ 𝐺 ℎ G h\in\textit{G} .

If g is not an element of G, InnerAutomorphism returns fail; in this case, the isomorphism from G to G^g induced by conjugation with g can be constructed with ConjugatorIsomorphism (40.6-1) or with ConjugatorAutomorphism (40.6-2).

InnerAutomorphismNC does the same as InnerAutomorphism, except that the check is omitted whether 𝑔 ∈ 𝐺 g G \textit{g}\in\textit{G} .
