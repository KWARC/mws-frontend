66 Polynomials and Rational Functions

Let R 𝑅 R be a commutative ring-with-one. We call a free associative algebra A 𝐴 A over R 𝑅 R a polynomial ring over R 𝑅 R . The free generators of A 𝐴 A are called indeterminates (to avoid naming conflicts with the word variables which will be used to denote GAP variables only) , they are usually denoted by x 1 , x 2 , … subscript 𝑥 1 subscript 𝑥 2 … x_{1},x_{2},\ldots . The number of indeterminates is called the rank of A 𝐴 A . The elements of A 𝐴 A are called polynomials. Products of indeterminates are called monomials, every polynomial can be expressed as a finite sum of products of monomials with ring elements in a form like r 1 , 0 ⁢ x 1 + r 1 , 1 ⁢ x 1 ⁢ x 2 + r 0 , 1 ⁢ x 2 + ⋯ subscript 𝑟 1 0 subscript 𝑥 1 subscript 𝑟 1 1 subscript 𝑥 1 subscript 𝑥 2 subscript 𝑟 0 1 subscript 𝑥 2 ⋯ r_{{1,0}}x_{1}+r_{{1,1}}x_{1}x_{2}+r_{{0,1}}x_{2}+\cdots with r i , j ∈ R subscript 𝑟 𝑖 𝑗 𝑅 r_{{i,j}}\in R .

A polynomial ring of rank 1 is called an univariate polynomial ring, its elements are univariate polynomials.

Polynomial rings of smaller rank naturally embed in rings of higher rank; if S 𝑆 S is a subring of R 𝑅 R then a polynomial ring over S 𝑆 S naturally embeds in a polynomial ring over R 𝑅 R of the same rank. Note however that GAP does not consider R 𝑅 R as a subset of a polynomial ring over R 𝑅 R ; for example the zero of R 𝑅 R ( 0 0 ) and the zero of the polynomial ring ( 0 ⁢ x 0 0 superscript 𝑥 0 0x^{0} ) are different objects.

Internally, indeterminates are represented by positive integers, but it is possible to give names to them to have them printed in a nicer way. Beware, however that there is not necessarily any relation between the way an indeterminate is called and the way it is printed. See section 66.1 for details.

If R 𝑅 R is an integral domain, the polynomial ring A 𝐴 A over R 𝑅 R is an integral domain as well and one can therefore form its quotient field Q 𝑄 Q . This field is called a field of rational functions. Again A 𝐴 A embeds naturally into Q 𝑄 Q and GAP will perform this embedding implicitly. (In fact it implements the ring of rational functions over R 𝑅 R .) To avoid problems with leading coefficients, however, R 𝑅 R must be a unique factorization domain.
