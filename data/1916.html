42 Permutations

GAP offers a data type permutation to describe the elements of permutation groups.

The points on which permutations in GAP act are the positive integers up to a certain architecture dependent limit, and the image of a point i 𝑖 i under a permutation p 𝑝 p is written i p superscript 𝑖 𝑝 i^{p} , which is expressed as i 𝑖 i ^ p 𝑝 p in GAP. (This action is also implemented by the function OnPoints (41.2-1).) If i 𝑖 i ^ p 𝑝 p is different from i 𝑖 i , we say that i 𝑖 i is moved by  p 𝑝 p , otherwise it is fixed. Permutations in GAP are entered and displayed in cycle notation, such as (1,2,3)(4,5).

The preimage of the point i 𝑖 i under the permutation p 𝑝 p can be computed as i 𝑖 i / p 𝑝 p , without constructing the inverse of p 𝑝 p .

For arithmetic operations for permutations and their precedence, see 31.12.

In the names of the GAP functions that deal with permutations, the word "Permutation" is usually abbreviated to "Perm", to save typing. For example, the category test function for permutations is IsPerm (42.1-1).
