71.12-7 ClassMultiplicationCoefficient
‣ ClassMultiplicationCoefficient( tbl, i, j, k )( operation )

returns the class multiplication coefficient of the classes i, j, and k of the group G 𝐺 G with ordinary character table tbl.

The class multiplication coefficient c i , j , k subscript 𝑐 𝑖 𝑗 𝑘 c_{{i,j,k}} of the classes i, j, k equals the number of pairs ( x , y ) 𝑥 𝑦 (x,y) of elements x , y ∈ G 𝑥 𝑦 𝐺 x,y\in G such that x 𝑥 x lies in class i, y 𝑦 y lies in class j, and their product x ⁢ y 𝑥 𝑦 xy is a fixed element of class k.

In the center of the group algebra of G 𝐺 G , these numbers are found as coefficients of the decomposition of the product of two class sums K i subscript 𝐾 𝑖 K_{i} and K j subscript 𝐾 𝑗 K_{j} into class sums:

\[ K_i K_j = \sum_k c_{ijk} K_k . \]

Given the character table of a finite group G 𝐺 G , whose classes are C 1 , … , C r subscript 𝐶 1 … subscript 𝐶 𝑟 C_{1},\ldots,C_{r} with representatives g i ∈ C i subscript 𝑔 𝑖 subscript 𝐶 𝑖 g_{i}\in C_{i} , the class multiplication coefficient c i ⁢ j ⁢ k subscript 𝑐 𝑖 𝑗 𝑘 c_{ijk} can be computed with the following formula:

\[ c_{ijk} = |C_i| \cdot |C_j| / |G| \cdot \sum_{{\chi \in Irr(G)}} \chi(g_i) \chi(g_j) \chi(g_k^{{-1}}) / \chi(1). \]

On the other hand the knowledge of the class multiplication coefficients admits the computation of the irreducible characters of G 𝐺 G , see IrrDixonSchneider (71.14-1).
