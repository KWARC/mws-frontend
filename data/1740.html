58.3 Galois Action

Let L ⁢ & ⁢ g ⁢ t ; K 𝐿 & 𝑔 𝑡 𝐾 L&gt;K be a field extension of finite degree. Then to each element α ∈ L 𝛼 𝐿 \alpha\in L , we can associate a K 𝐾 K -linear mapping φ α subscript 𝜑 𝛼 \varphi_{\alpha} on L 𝐿 L , and for a fixed K 𝐾 K -basis of L 𝐿 L , we can associate to α 𝛼 \alpha the matrix M α subscript 𝑀 𝛼 M_{\alpha} (over K 𝐾 K ) of this mapping.

The norm of α 𝛼 \alpha is defined as the determinant of M α subscript 𝑀 𝛼 M_{\alpha} , the trace of α 𝛼 \alpha is defined as the trace of M α subscript 𝑀 𝛼 M_{\alpha} , the minimal polynomial μ α subscript 𝜇 𝛼 \mu_{\alpha} and the trace polynomial χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} of α 𝛼 \alpha are defined as the minimal polynomial (see 58.3-2) and the characteristic polynomial (see CharacteristicPolynomial (24.13-1) and TracePolynomial (58.3-3)) of M α subscript 𝑀 𝛼 M_{\alpha} . (Note that μ α subscript 𝜇 𝛼 \mu_{\alpha} depends only on K 𝐾 K whereas χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} depends on both L 𝐿 L and K 𝐾 K .)

Thus norm and trace of α 𝛼 \alpha are elements of K 𝐾 K , and μ α subscript 𝜇 𝛼 \mu_{\alpha} and χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} are polynomials over K 𝐾 K , χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} being a power of μ α subscript 𝜇 𝛼 \mu_{\alpha} , and the degree of χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} equals the degree of the field extension L ⁢ & ⁢ g ⁢ t ; K 𝐿 & 𝑔 𝑡 𝐾 L&gt;K .

The conjugates of α 𝛼 \alpha in L 𝐿 L are those roots of χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} (with multiplicity) that lie in L 𝐿 L ; note that if only L 𝐿 L is given, there is in general no way to access the roots outside L 𝐿 L .

Analogously, the Galois group of the extension L ⁢ & ⁢ g ⁢ t ; K 𝐿 & 𝑔 𝑡 𝐾 L&gt;K is defined as the group of all those field automorphisms of L 𝐿 L that fix K 𝐾 K pointwise.

If L ⁢ & ⁢ g ⁢ t ; K 𝐿 & 𝑔 𝑡 𝐾 L&gt;K is a Galois extension then the conjugates of α 𝛼 \alpha are all roots of χ α subscript 𝜒 𝛼 \chi_{\alpha} (with multiplicity), the set of conjugates equals the roots of μ α subscript 𝜇 𝛼 \mu_{\alpha} , the norm of α 𝛼 \alpha equals the product and the trace of α 𝛼 \alpha equals the sum of the conjugates of α 𝛼 \alpha , and the Galois group in the sense of the above definition equals the usual Galois group,

Note that MinimalPolynomial( F, z ) is a polynomial over F, whereas Norm( F, z ) is the norm of the element z in F w.r.t. the field extension F > LeftActingDomain( F ).

The default methods for field elements are as follows. MinimalPolynomial (66.8-1) solves a system of linear equations, TracePolynomial (58.3-3) computes the appropriate power of the minimal polynomial, Norm (58.3-4) and Trace (58.3-5) values are obtained as coefficients of the characteristic polynomial, and Conjugates (58.3-6) uses the factorization of the characteristic polynomial.

For elements in finite fields and cyclotomic fields, one wants to do the computations in a different way since the field extensions in question are Galois extensions, and the Galois groups are well-known in these cases. More general, if a field is in the category IsFieldControlledByGaloisGroup then the default methods are the following. Conjugates (58.3-6) returns the sorted list of images (with multiplicity) of the element under the Galois group, Norm (58.3-4) computes the product of the conjugates, Trace (58.3-5) computes the sum of the conjugates, TracePolynomial (58.3-3) and MinimalPolynomial (66.8-1) compute the product of linear factors x - c 𝑥 𝑐 x-c with c 𝑐 c ranging over the conjugates and the set of conjugates, respectively.
