56.7-3 GcdRepresentation
‣ GcdRepresentation( [R, ]r1, r2, ... )( function )
‣ GcdRepresentation( [R, ]list )( function )

GcdRepresentation returns a representation of the greatest common divisor of the ring elements r1, r2, … … \ldots resp. of the ring elements in the list list in the Euclidean ring R, if given, and otherwise in their default ring, see DefaultRing (56.1-3).

A representation of the gcd g 𝑔 g of the elements r 1 , r 2 , … subscript 𝑟 1 subscript 𝑟 2 … r_{1},r_{2},\ldots of a ring R 𝑅 R is a list of ring elements s 1 , s 2 , … subscript 𝑠 1 subscript 𝑠 2 … s_{1},s_{2},\ldots of R 𝑅 R , such that g = s 1 ⁢ r 1 + s 2 ⁢ r 2 + ⋯ 𝑔 subscript 𝑠 1 subscript 𝑟 1 subscript 𝑠 2 subscript 𝑟 2 ⋯ g=s_{1}r_{1}+s_{2}r_{2}+\cdots . Such representations do not exist in all rings, but they do exist in Euclidean rings (see IsEuclideanRing (56.6-1)), which can be shown using the Euclidean algorithm, which in fact can compute those coefficients.

gap> a:= Indeterminate( Rationals, "a" );;
gap> GcdRepresentation( a^2+1, a^3+1 );
[ -1/2*a^2-1/2*a+1/2, 1/2*a+1/2 ]

Gcdex (14.3-5) provides similar functionality over the integers.
