64.14-3 ObjByExtRep
‣ ObjByExtRep( F, descr )( method )

An UEALattice element is represented by a list of the form [ m1, c1, m2, c2, ... ], where the c1, c2 etc. are coefficients, and the m1, m2 etc. are monomials. A monomial is a list of the form [ ind1, e1, ind2, e2, ... ] where ind1, ind2 are indices, and e1, e2 etc. are exponents. Let N 𝑁 N be the number of positive roots of the underlying Lie algebra L. The indices lie between 1 and d ⁢ i ⁢ m ⁢ ( L ) 𝑑 𝑖 𝑚 𝐿 dim(L) . If an index lies between 1 and N, then it represents a negative root vector (corresponding to the root NegativeRoots( R )[ind], where R is the root system of L; see NegativeRoots (64.6-8)). This leads to a factor yind1^(e1) in the printed form of the monomial (which equals z^e1/e1!, where z is a basis element of L). If an index lies between N + 1 𝑁 1 N+1 and 2 ⁢ N 2 𝑁 2N , then it represents a positive root vector. Finally, if ind lies between 2 ⁢ N + 1 2 𝑁 1 2N+1 and 2 ⁢ N + r ⁢ a ⁢ n ⁢ k 2 𝑁 𝑟 𝑎 𝑛 𝑘 2N+rank , then it represents an element of the Cartan subalgebra. This is printed as ( h 1 / e 1 ) subscript ℎ 1 subscript 𝑒 1 (h_{1}/e_{1}) , meaning ( h 1 e 1 ) binomial subscript ℎ 1 subscript 𝑒 1 {h_{1}\choose e_{1}} , where h 1 , … , h r ⁢ a ⁢ n ⁢ k subscript ℎ 1 … subscript ℎ 𝑟 𝑎 𝑛 𝑘 h_{1},\ldots,h_{rank} are the canonical Cartan generators.

The zero element is represented by the empty list, the identity element by the list [ [], 1 ].
