25.2 Normal Forms over the Integers

This section describes the computation of the Hermite and Smith normal form of integer matrices.

The Hermite Normal Form (HNF) H 𝐻 H of an integer matrix A 𝐴 A is a row equivalent upper triangular form such that all off-diagonal entries are reduced modulo the diagonal entry of the column they are in. There exists a unique unimodular matrix Q 𝑄 Q such that Q ⁢ A = H 𝑄 𝐴 𝐻 QA=H .

The Smith Normal Form S 𝑆 S of an integer matrix A 𝐴 A is the unique equivalent diagonal form with S i subscript 𝑆 𝑖 S_{i} dividing S j subscript 𝑆 𝑗 S_{j} for i ⁢ & ⁢ l ⁢ t ; j 𝑖 & 𝑙 𝑡 𝑗 i&lt;j . There exist unimodular integer matrices P , Q 𝑃 𝑄 P,Q such that P ⁢ A ⁢ Q = S 𝑃 𝐴 𝑄 𝑆 PAQ=S .
